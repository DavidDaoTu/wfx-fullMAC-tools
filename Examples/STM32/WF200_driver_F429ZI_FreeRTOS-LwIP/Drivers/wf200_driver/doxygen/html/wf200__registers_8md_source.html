1 WF200 registers {#wf200_registers}
2 ============
3 
4 This section presents the registers exposed by WF200 to the host. The section focuses on the registers involved in the WF200 FMAC driver.
5 Those registers can be accessed both using SPI or SDIO.
6 
7 ## SPI
8 In SPI, the MSB is used to define the following data as read or write (1: read, 0: write). The next three bits are the actual register address, and all other bits define the length of the following transmission.
9 Each command word is 16 bits long.
10 
11 |**Bit number** | 15 | 14 downto 12 | 11 downto 0 |
12 |----------------|-----|--------------|-------------|
13 | **Function** | R/W | Address | Length |
14 
15 Each command for this operational mode is formatted as described below.
16 ![SPI access to WF200 registers](@ref spi_register_communication.png)
17 For each command, there can be data in or data out, and data can be any multiple of 16 bit blocks.
18 
19 ## SDIO
20 In SDIO, these registers are defined for command 53 (::wf200_host_sdio_transfer_cmd53). The addresses below are used as the first 6 bits of SDIO command address field.
